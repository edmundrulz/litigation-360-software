# BUSINESS CONTINUITY PLAN

## Purpose

This document defines how Litigation 360 and the legal practice continue operating during disruption.

## Minimum Firm Operations

The firm must be able to continue:

1. Accessing client files
2. Accessing matter records
3. Checking court deadlines
4. Communicating with clients
5. Preparing urgent filings
6. Approving urgent legal actions
7. Recording payments
8. Tracking staff and runner actions

## Critical Services

| Service | Criticality | Maximum Downtime |
|---|---|---|
| Court deadlines | Critical | 0-2 hours |
| Client documents | Critical | 4 hours |
| Matter database | Critical | 2 hours |
| Email | High | 4 hours |
| Billing | Medium | 24 hours |
| Reports | Medium | 24 hours |

## Continuity Rule

If normal operations fail, the firm moves to emergency mode.

Emergency mode means:

- Manual registers may be used
- Owner must be notified
- Lawyer must approve legal actions
- All temporary records must later be uploaded into Litigation 360
